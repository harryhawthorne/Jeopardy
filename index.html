Jeopardy! Data Visualisations

A collection of interactive charts exploring Jeopardy! data. Hover over chart elements to see relevant clues and answers.

The data was collected through webscraping and contains 500,000+ clues and answers.

Hardest Categories Network Graph

This graph shows the semantic relationships between the most difficult categories in Jeopardy!, filtered to include only those with at least 25 Triple Stumpers over the shows history. We use the sentence-transformer model all-MiniLM-L6-v2 to create vector embeddings from the text of all Triple Stumper clues and answers within each category. We then use cosine similarity between these vectors to link the most similar categories. Larger nodes represent categories with a higher proportion of Triple Stumpers to total clues.

Top Answers Bump Chart

This bump chart tracks the changing rank of the most frequently occurring answers over the seasons. It shows how some of the most common answers like 'Australia' and 'Canada' have been relatively common for lots of seasons, while others rise and fall in popularity.

Year Mention Frequency Chart

This chart analyses the text of all clues, looking for any four-digit numbers that we can interpret as a year. The frequency of each year is plotted to show which historical periods are most referenced in Jeopardy!.

Answers by Country World Map

This map displays the frequency of answers that are simply a country name.

Answers by US State Map

This map shows the frequency of answers that are US state names.

Answers by Chemical Element

This visualisation displays the frequency of answers that are elements from the periodic table.
