Jeopardy! Data Visualisations

A collection of interactive charts exploring Jeopardy! data.

Data was collected using a webscraper and contains over 500,000 clues and answers.

Network of hardest categories

This graph shows the semantic relationships between the most difficult categories in Jeopardy!, filtered to include only those with at least 25 Triple Stumpers. We use the sentence-transformer model all-MiniLM-L6-v2 to create vector embeddings from the text of all Triple Stumper clues and answers within each category. We then use cosine similarity between these vectors to link the most similar categories. Larger nodes represent categories with a higher proportion of Triple Stumpers to total clues.

Top answers by season

This bump chart tracks the changing rank of the most frequently occurring answers over the seasons. It shows how some of the most common answers like 'Australia' and 'Canada' have been relatively common for lots of seasons, while others rise and fall in popularity.

Years mentioned in clues

This chart analyses the text of all clues, looking for any four-digit numbers that we can interpret as a year. The frequency of each year is plotted to show which historical periods are most referenced in Jeopardy!.

Answers from around the world

This map displays the frequency of answers that are simply a country name.

Answers from around the US

This map shows the frequency of answers that are US state names.

Answers on the Periodic Table

This visualisation displays the frequency of answers that are elements from the periodic table.
